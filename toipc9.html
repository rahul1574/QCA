FULL SUBTRACTOR QCA

A full subtractor is a digital circuit that subtracts one binary number from another, taking into account a borrow bit from a previous stage. In Quantum-dot Cellular Automata (QCA), constructing a full subtractor involves using basic QCA components such as majority gates and inverters. Here's a detailed look at how a full subtractor works in QCA:

Components Used

1.Majority Gates: Used for implementing logic operations.

2.Inverters: Used for signal inversion where necessary.

In QCA, a full subtractor is constructed using majority gates and inverters to perform the XOR, AND, and OR operations required for the difference and borrow-out outputs, respectively. By leveraging the Coulombic interactions and the inherent properties of QCA cells, the full subtractor effectively performs binary subtraction at the nanoscale, enabling the creation of more complex digital circuits.
