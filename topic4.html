MAJORITY GATE QCA

In Quantum-dot Cellular Automata (QCA), the majority gate is a fundamental logic element that performs a majority voting function on three input signals. The majority gate outputs the value that is in the majority among its three inputs. This gate can be used to implement various logic functions, including AND, OR, and the inverter (NOT gate) when combined with fixed inputs.

Structure of a Majority Gate
    1. QCA Cells Arrangement
  • The majority gate typically consists of five QCA cells arranged in a cross pattern:
  • Three input cells (A, B, and C)
  • One central cell (M)
  • One output cell (O)
    2. Positioning:
  • The three input cells (A, B, and C) are placed at three corners of a cross.
  • The central cell (M) is at the intersection of the inputs
  • The output cell (O) is at the fourth corner, directly influenced by the central cell (M).
Working Principle

1. Input Polarizations: - Each input cell can be in one of two polarization states, representing binary 0 or 1.

2. Electron Configuration: - The polarization of each input cell influences the electron configuration of the central cell (M) due to Coulombic interactions.

3. Majority Voting: - The central cell (M) adopts a polarization that reflects the majority of the input cells. If at least two of the three input cells have the same polarization, the central cell will also adopt that polarization.

- The output cell (O) then takes on the polarization of the central cell (M), thus representing the majority value of the inputs.

Output equation for majority gate will be M=AB+BC+CA.

Implementing Logic Functions with Majority Gate

1. AND Gate:

- Fix one of the inputs (C) to 0.

- The majority gate equation becomes M (A, B, 0) = A .B

As per the equation of majority gate, we get

M (A, B, 0) = AB+B(0)+(0)A

M (A, B, 0) = AB

2. OR Gate:

- Fix one of the inputs (C) to 1.

- The majority gate equation becomes M (A, B, 1) = A + B

As per the equation of majority gate, we get

M (A, B, 1) = AB+B(1)+(1)A

M (A, B, 1) = AB+A+B

M (A, B, 1) = A+B (from AB+A=A)
